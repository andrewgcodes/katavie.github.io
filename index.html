Hey, I'm Kat, a high schooler from Lowell, MA, and incoming freshman at MIT. Above all else, I value integrity, respect, and open-mindedness.

I started Science & Us, a nonprofit that makes science more approachable. We hold events to help students make multimedia communication projects and learn about careers bridging STEM (science, technology, engineering, math) and the public.

I’m co-captain of FIRST Robotics Competition Team 5962, and was the lead organizer of MAHacks, an entrepreneurship-focused high school hackathon in greater Boston. I’m among the 0.9% of applicants to earn the NCWIT National Aspirations in Computing Award in 2019.

I’ve conducted scientific research at Harvard University, the University of Massachusetts Lowell, and independently. I’ve presented bioinformatics and biotechnology projects at the National Junior Science and Humanities Symposium and Massachusetts Science and Engineering Fair.

Some of my other interests include writing, hand lettering, and applying data science to fields like sociology. When I'm not working on projects, I'm probably scrolling through memes or exercising—maybe both.
