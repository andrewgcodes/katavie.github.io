Hey, I'm Kat, a high schooler from Lowell, MA, and incoming freshman at MIT, where I plan to study Course 6-14 (Computer Science, Economics, Data Science). Above all else, I value integrity, kindness, and gratitude.

I started Science & Us, a nonprofit making science more approachable for everyone. At our Makeathons, 50 high schoolers create multimedia projects communicating STEM (science, technology, engineering, math) topics to diverse audiences. By pairing students with mentors, we open up fields like science policy, journalism, and education, which are fulfilling and essential but rarely mentioned in school.

I'm lead organizer of MAHacks, a high school hackathon that aims to enable participants to turn their weekend ideas into lasting projects, and co-captain of FIRST Robotics Competition Team 5962. I'm among the 0.9% of applicants to earn the NCWIT National Aspirations in Computing Award in 2019.

I’ve conducted scientific research at Harvard University, the University of Massachusetts Lowell, and independently. I’ve presented bioinformatics and biotechnology projects at the National Junior Science and Humanities Symposium, Massachusetts Science and Engineering Fair, and Journal of Emerging Investigators.

When I'm not working on projects, I'm probably scrolling through memes or exercising—maybe both.
